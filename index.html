About Me

Hi there! Innovating at the intersection of statistics and health, I’m a Ph.D. student in Statistics passionate about advancing health research and improving patient outcomes through robust causal inference. My work focuses on developing scalable Bayesian Double Machine Learning (BDML) methods to estimate treatment effects in high-dimensional, real-world healthcare data. I’m especially interested in tackling the challenges of bias, uncertainty, and interpretability in causal inference, where traditional models and standalone machine learning often fall short. By combining statistical rigor with practical relevance, I aim to generate insights that support more personalized and evidence-based healthcare.

Projects

  • Bayesian Estimation of Reproduction Numbers (Master’s Thesis):   Developed classical and empirical Bayesian estimators for reproduction numbers using outbreak size distributions modeled via branching processes. Proposed and implemented monotonization techniques (Van Houwelingen & Isotonic Regression) to improve estimator stability. This study showed that monotonized empirical Bayes estimators significantly reduce estimation variability ("jumpiness") and outperform classical methods in simulation, enhancing the reliability of epidemic forecasting tools.
  • Comparative Modeling of Asthma Drug Efficacy:  Analyzed longitudinal FEV data from asthma patients using mixed models and GEE. Compared repeated measures, multilevel linear regression, and GEE approaches with different covariance structures. This project identified strengths and limitations of different modeling approaches (mixed models, multilevel models, GEE) and concluded the repeated measures model as most appropriate for the data, informing better model selection in clinical settings.
  • Survival Analysis in Leukemia Transplant Outcomes:  Used Cox Proportional Hazards modeling to investigate predictors of acute graft-versus-host disease in bone marrow transplant patients. Focused on evaluating methotrexate’s effect as prophylaxis. This project contributes insights into treatment evaluation and the design of future transplant protocols.
  • Breast Cancer Diagnosis Using ML:  Compared traditional machine learning algorithms for classifying breast cancer tumors using the benchmark WBCD dataset. Identified Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) and KNN as the most effective methods, achieving F1-scores and balanced accuracy >97%. Demonstrated the potential of ML in clinical diagnostic tools.
  • Deep Learning for COVID-19 Tweet Sentiment Analysis:  Built an ensemble deep learning model to classify public sentiment toward COVID-19 using Twitter data. Improved classification accuracy by leveraging a stacking ensemble (CNN + RNN + BiLSTM with Random Forest meta-classifier). 
  • Tinnitus Intervention Study: Regression Modeling:  Modeled the effect of treatment and psychosocial factors on tinnitus improvement using multiple regression and modern variable selection techniques. Selected the best-performing predictive model using cross-validation and diagnostics. Provided actionable insights into predictors of treatment success in tinnitus therapy.
